My Research

What I Do

My research interests center on applications to personnel selection and applied psychometrics, including testing, assessment, and measurement, especially as related to personality. Specifically, there is a focus on the measurement of implicit personality via conditional reasoning, as well as various questions related to this measurement system. Additionally, there exists a broad interest in research methodology, both in terms of advancing my own expertise using various methods (e.g. MLM, LPA, Meta-Analysis), and also implementing these methods in order to try and uniquely answer I-O psychology and management relevant questions.

Testing & Assessment

How can we deter and/or detect faking on assessments for personnel selection? Can we predict when response distortion is most likely? Which assessments are appropriate for cross-cultural use? How do decision aids affect hiring decisions?

Personality

How do implicit and explicit personality interact to uniquely predict work-related outcomes? Do profiles of aggressive personality exist? Can we leverage the need for achievement in work contexts beyond performance outcomes? What is the relationship between various aspects of personality and police misconduct?

Research Methods

How can person centered methods be used to answer I-O and management relevant questions? Can configurations help us better explain certain phenomena?
